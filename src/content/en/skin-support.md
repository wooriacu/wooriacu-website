Chronic skin conditions—such as eczema, psoriasis, and persistent rashes—are often reflections of the body's internal health and metabolic balance. In traditional Eastern medicine, we view the skin as an external mirror of our internal system. When internal metabolic stasis accumulates or essential hydrating fluids become depleted, the skin barrier may struggle to maintain its natural defenses. Our approach focuses on clearing internal heat, soothing systemic inflammation, and supporting your body's innate resilience.

## 1. Traditional Insights into Chronic Skin Conditions

- **Damp-Heat Accumulation:** Inflammatory rashes often arise from an internal accumulation of moisture and heat. When metabolic processes are sluggish, this "damp-heat" can manifest as redness, localized swelling, and persistent discomfort.

- **Blood Dryness and Malnourishment:** Chronic, scaly conditions are often linked to a depletion of nourishing fluids (Yin and Blood). Lacking deep internal moisture, the skin can become brittle, leading to compromised barrier integrity.

- **Systemic Sensitivity:** Sudden flare-ups may indicate that the body's superficial protective energy (Wei Qi) is compromised, making the skin highly reactive to environmental factors.
