Menstruation is a vital reflection of systemic health. While minor shifts are common, recurring irregularities can be a meaningful signal that the body's internal balance requires attention. In Korean medicine, we view menstrual rhythm as an outcome of the vitality of internal organ systems and the smooth circulation of Qi and Blood.

## 1. Types of Menstrual Irregularity

- **Polymenorrhea:** Cycles occurring significantly earlier than the typical interval.

- **Oligomenorrhea:** Cycles delayed beyond the typical interval.

- **Hypomenorrhea (Scanty):** Menstrual duration or volume that is notably sparse.

- **Hypermenorrhea (Heavy):** Menstrual duration or volume that is excessively prolonged or heavy.

- **Amenorrhea:** The absence of menstruation for an extended period.

- **Irregular Spotting:** Inconsistent uterine bleeding unrelated to the standard cycle.

## 2. Understanding the Root Causes in Korean Medicine

- **Nutritional Deficiency & Depleted Qi/Blood:** Insufficient nourishment or overwork can deprive the uterine system of essential support, leading to irregular cycles.

- **Accumulation of Metabolic Stagnation (Seub-dam):** An imbalance in metabolic processing can lead to a state of sluggish internal flow, potentially impacting the uterine environment.

- **Endocrine Stability:** The menstrual rhythm relies on a sophisticated internal balance. External stressors and lifestyle factors can impact this delicate harmony.

- **Stress-Induced Heat:** Chronic stress can lead to a buildup of heat in the upper body and relative coldness in the lower abdomen (Sang-yeol-ha-han), disrupting the flow of energy needed for a healthy cycle.

- **Pelvic Coldness:** When the lower abdomen lacks vital warming energy, circulation slows, leading to a dull, heavy sensation and potentially impacting menstrual flow and comfort.

## 3. Wellness Habits for Menstrual Health

- Maintain a balanced, nutritious diet with regular meals.

- Minimize cold or processed foods to support metabolic health.

- Manage stress through mindfulness and adequate rest.

- Keep the abdominal area warm to support comfortable pelvic circulation.

- Engage in consistent, moderate exercise to promote healthy blood flow.
