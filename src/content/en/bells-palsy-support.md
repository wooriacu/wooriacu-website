Facial paralysis is clinically approached by evaluating both external triggers and internal physiological status. For effective recovery, we focus on an integrated, constitutional approach.

## 1. Primary Factors

- **External Factors (Wind-Cold):** The condition often occurs following exposure to cold drafts, cold-natured environments, or in conjunction with viral triggers.

- **Internal Deficiencies:** Exposure to these factors impacts those whose natural defenses are already compromised by chronic fatigue, nutritional imbalance, or accumulated stress.

## 2. Lifestyle Guidance

- **Protection:** Keep the affected area warm and avoid direct drafts. Maintain a stable core body temperature by avoiding excessively cold foods or beverages.

- **Stabilization:** Facial paralysis often reflects systemic exhaustion. Prioritize deep rest, sufficient sleep, and emotional peace to support the body's healing.

## 3. Clinical Modalities at Woori Acupuncture

- **Constitutional Herbal Support:** We identify internal deficiencies to prescribe tailored herbal formulas designed to reinforce vital energy and blood circulation.

- **Acupuncture Therapy:** Targeted acupuncture is utilized to stimulate nerve pathways and encourage smooth circulation in the affected facial areas.

- **Facial Thermal Therapy:** Gentle heat applications help mitigate localized coldness and promote blood flow.

## 4. Recovery Guidance

When proactive acupuncture care and lifestyle modifications are implemented, our goal is to support the body's natural healing capacity to facilitate a smoother recovery process. Recovery rates may vary depending on individual constitution and severity; therefore, consistent, meticulous care is highly recommended to maximize long-term health outcomes.
