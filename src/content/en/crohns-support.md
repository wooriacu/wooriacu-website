Crohn's disease is a complex chronic inflammatory bowel disease (IBD). From the perspective of Traditional Korean Medicine, we approach this condition by assessing how individual constitutional vulnerabilities and lifestyle factors impact the digestive system's stability. Our goal is to support your body's natural harmony alongside your standard medical care.

## 1. Understanding Functional Patterns

Gastrointestinal distress often manifests in patterns related to your body's unique constitution.

- **Pattern A (Digestive Irritation):** Often associated with sensitivity to specific dietary triggers, manifesting as bloating or abdominal discomfort.

- **Pattern B (Systemic Coldness):** Associated with internal coldness in the gut, which may affect motility and absorption.

## 2. Supportive Lifestyle & Management

- **Mindful Temperature:** Avoid excessively cold or iced beverages, which may cause spasms in the intestinal muscles. Opt for warm or lukewarm liquids.

- **Core Warmth:** Protecting the abdomen from cold environments and using warm compresses can help maintain digestive comfort.

- **Individualized Dietary Approach:** Dietary triggers vary significantly between patients. Rather than following generalized restrictive diets, we encourage you to work with your healthcare team to identify your personal triggers and ensure adequate nutritional intake.

## 3. Integrative Care at Woori Acupuncture Clinic

- **Constitutional Herbal Support:** We assess your unique inflammatory stage and constitution to prescribe herbal formulas designed to support digestive vitality and help create a favorable environment for mucosal stability.

- **Acupuncture:** We utilize acupuncture to assist in relieving muscular tension and supporting autonomic balance, aiming to help the body maintain a more stable and comfortable digestive state.

## 4. A Note on Medication and Care

**Important:** Please do not discontinue or modify any prescribed medications (such as anti-inflammatories, immunosuppressants, or biologics) without consulting your primary gastroenterologist. Our supportive care is intended to be integrated into your existing treatment plan, not to replace it.
