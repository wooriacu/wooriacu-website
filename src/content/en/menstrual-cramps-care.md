Traditional Eastern medicine views smooth, pain-free menstruation as a primary indicator of balanced Qi and Blood dynamics. When pelvic circulation becomes restricted due to systemic cold or emotional strain, the body struggles to maintain healthy flow, leading to sharp cramping, lower back aching, and premenstrual fatigue. ## 1. Eastern Medical Insights into Menstrual Cramps

- **Stagnation of Blood and Pelvic Congestion (어혈, 瘀血):** Chronic stress, cold exposure, or structural tension can cause blood flow within the pelvic cavity to become sluggish and stagnant. In Eastern medicine, this is known as Blood Stasis. When circulation pools, the uterine muscles must contract more aggressively to clear the lining, resulting in sharp, throbbing cramps, lower abdominal pressure, and dark spotting.

- **Accumulation of Cold in the Lower Uterine Vessel (하초냉증, 下焦冷症):** The delicate reproductive system is highly sensitive to temperature. When Cold penetrates the lower abdomen—often exacerbated by poor circulation or constitutional weakness—it constricts the local blood vessels and tightens pelvic ligaments. This cold-induced restriction causes a dull, heavy, aching pain that typically feels worse in cold environments and significantly improves with the application of soothing warmth.

- **Liver Qi Stagnation and Emotional Sensitivity (간기울결, 肝氣鬱結):** The Liver meridian is traditionally responsible for regulating the smooth flow of both blood volume and emotional energy. When chronic stress, frustration, or lack of rest causes this energy to bottle up (Liver Qi stagnation), it directly disrupts the uterine pathways. This emotional-physical bottleneck often manifests as intense premenstrual syndrome (PMS), breast tenderness, mood swings, and severe cramping right as the cycle begins.
