## 1. Understanding Acne and Skin Balance

Acne often reflects internal systemic balance as well as surface conditions. Without addressing the underlying systemic and environmental factors, skin presentation can frequently fluctuate. Our holistic approach focuses on harmonizing the body's internal environment, helping the skin maintain its natural clarity and structural resilience from within.

## 2. Constitutional and Internal Factors

Everyone possesses a unique physical template, meaning skin distress can manifest differently based on your constitution:

- **Tendencies Toward Internal Heat:** Often associated with reactive or sensitive blemishes that benefit from cooling and soothing support.

- **Tendencies Toward Stagnant Circulation:** May lead to a slower recovery process, making the skin baseline more vulnerable to lingering marks. Adjusting daily habits according to your specific constitutional needs is an essential element of long-term skin maintenance.

## 3. Diet, Nutrition, and Lifestyle Guidelines

Digestive health is closely linked to sebum balance and skin vitality. While individual dietary needs vary, many find it helpful to mindfully limit the following:

- Highly processed, packaged, or fast-food selections.

- Excessively greasy, fried, or highly refined sugary foods.

- Excessive caffeine or intensely stimulating, overly spicy dishes.

- Artificial additives and chemical preservatives.

## 4. Seasonal, Environmental, and Secondary Triggers

- **Environmental Factors:** Weather shifts, humidity, or aggressive skin care routines can compromise the epidermal barrier.

- **Stress and Fatigue:** Prolonged stress and inadequate sleep can disrupt the circulation of vital energy (Qi) and blood, often causing temporary heat to rise toward the face.

- **External Irritation:** Physical friction or systemic impact from certain external factors can influence the skin's baseline ecology.
