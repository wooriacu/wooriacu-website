A stroke is a significant neurological event that requires immediate emergency medical intervention. Once the acute phase is stabilized and conventional care has been established, the rehabilitation phase begins. At Woori Acupuncture, we provide integrative support to help optimize this recovery window by improving systemic circulation, enhancing neuro-muscular feedback, and supporting the body's natural neuroplasticity alongside your ongoing rehabilitation programs.

## 1. Traditional Insights into Post-Stroke Recovery

- **Clearing Meridian Obstruction:** In traditional frameworks, stroke sequelae are often understood as disruptions in the body's energy and blood pathways. Our care focuses on clearing these obstructions to support the body's ability to "re-establish" communication between the nervous system and the limbs.

- **Resolving Vascular Stasis:** Improving microcirculation is essential for delivering oxygen and nutrients to affected tissues. We focus on enhancing blood flow to help support the repair of tissues and peripheral function.

- **Replenishing Foundational Energy:** Stroke recovery is physically demanding. We focus on replenishing the patient's Qi and Yin reserves to ensure they possess the stamina required for consistent physical and occupational therapy.

## 2. Integrative Clinical Modalities at Woori Acupuncture

- **Constitutional Neural Support:** We provide customized herbal formulas tailored to your stage of recovery and current health markers. These are designed to nourish depleted neurological pathways and support circulatory health without interfering with your primary medications.

- **Neuro-Meridian Acupuncture:** We utilize precise acupuncture protocols designed to encourage neuromuscular re-education. This targeted care helps reduce spasticity, improves local circulation, and works in harmony with your conventional physical therapy to maximize functional gains.

**Important Note on Coordinated Care:** Post-stroke recovery is a marathon, not a sprint. Please **continue all rehabilitation exercises, medications, and treatments prescribed by your neurologist and physical therapist.** Our clinical role is to provide constitutional and supportive care that works **in tandem** with your established medical regimen to bolster your progress.
